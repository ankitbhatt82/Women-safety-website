Understanding Domestic Violence Against Women

Domestic violence against women refers to any pattern of behavior in a relationship that is used to gain or maintain power and control over a woman. This violence can manifest in various forms, including:

Physical Abuse

Hitting, slapping, punching, choking, or using weapons.

Emotional Abuse

Undermining an individual’s self-worth through constant criticism, name-calling, or damaging one's relationships with others.

Sexual Abuse

Coercing or attempting to coerce any sexual contact or behavior without consent.

Economic Abuse

Controlling or withholding financial resources to make the victim financially dependent.

Psychological Abuse

Intimidation, threats of harm, and isolation from friends, family, or other support systems.

Steps to Take If Someone Is Facing Domestic Violence

Recognize the Abuse

Understand and acknowledge that the behavior being experienced is abusive.

Document the Abuse

Keep a detailed record of instances of abuse, including dates, times, and descriptions of what happened. Photographs of injuries and copies of any threatening communications can be useful.

Reach Out for Support

Friends and Family Confide in trusted individuals who can provide emotional support and possibly a safe place to stay.

Hotlines and Helplines Contact national or local domestic violence hotlines.

Support Groups Join support groups for survivors of domestic violence, where sharing experiences can be therapeutic and offer practical advice.

Develop a Safety Plan

Plan an escape route from your home and a safe place to go.

Pack an emergency bag with essentials such as identification documents, money, medications, and clothes.

Memorize important phone numbers in case you cannot use your phone.

Seek Legal Help

Protection Orders: Obtain a restraining or protective order to legally prevent the abuser from coming near you.

Legal Counsel: Consult with a lawyer who specializes in domestic violence cases to understand your rights and options.

Access Professional Services

Healthcare Providers Seek medical attention for injuries and discuss the abuse with your doctor.

Counselors and Therapists Professional mental health services can help cope with the emotional and psychological impact of abuse.

Utilize Shelters and Resources

Many communities have shelters and organizations that provide temporary housing, food, and support services for victims of domestic violence.

Resources

Contact Authorities

In cases of immediate danger, call emergency services (Police 100).

For any emergency assistance please call women helpline number 112.

Report the abuse to local law enforcement to document the incidents and build a case if necessary.

For any issue related to Violence against Women Please Call on Helpline : 7827-170-170·

181 Women Helpline (WHL) is a 24-hour confidential service for women and child survivors and victims of any form of violence including domestic & intimate partner.

If you or someone you know is experiencing domestic violence, reaching out for help and taking these steps can be life-changing and potentially life-saving.
