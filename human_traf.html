Human Trafficking

Definition

Human trafficking is a form of modern-day slavery where individuals are exploited for commercial gain. It involves the use of force, fraud, or coercion to recruit, harbor, transport, or obtain a person for labor or sexual exploitation.

Forms of Human Trafficking

Human trafficking can take various forms, including:

Sex Trafficking: Involving the recruitment, harboring, transportation, provision, or obtaining of a person for the purpose of a commercial sex act, in which a commercial sex act is induced by force, fraud, or coercion, or in which the person induced to perform such an act is under the age of 18 years.

Labor Trafficking: Involving the recruitment, harboring, transportation, provision, or obtaining of a person for labor or services, through the use of force, fraud, or coercion for the purpose of subjection to involuntary servitude, peonage, debt bondage, or slavery.

What to Do

If someone is facing human trafficking, encourage them to reach out to authorities or organizations specialized in human trafficking support. Provide emotional support and reassurance that they are not alone. Help them access resources such as hotlines, shelters, and legal aid. Do not attempt to confront traffickers directly, as this can escalate the situation and endanger the victim. Report any suspicions of human trafficking to the appropriate authorities.

Prevention

Preventing human trafficking involves:

Education: Raising awareness about the signs and risks of human trafficking.

Support: Providing support and resources to vulnerable individuals and communities.

Legislation: Enacting and enforcing laws that criminalize human trafficking and protect victims.

Collaboration: Collaborating with governments, NGOs, and international organizations to combat human trafficking at local, national, and global levels.

Resources

Contact Authorities

In cases of immediate danger, call emergency services (police 100).

For any emergency assistance please call women helpline number 112.

Report the abuse to local law enforcement to document the incidents and build a case if necessary.
