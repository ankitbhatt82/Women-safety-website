Street Sexual Harassment

Street sexual harassment refers to unwanted and unwelcome behavior or comments of a sexual nature in public spaces. This can include catcalling, whistling, leering, groping, and making inappropriate sexual comments. Such behavior is invasive, intimidating, and can cause significant distress and fear.

What to Do If You Are Facing Street Sexual Harassment

Here are steps to take if you are facing street sexual harassment:

Stay Safe

Your safety is the most important thing. Try to stay in well-lit, populated areas and avoid engaging with the harasser if you feel threatened.

Speak Up

If you feel safe, you can assertively tell the harasser to stop. Use clear, strong language like "Stop harassing me!" or "Leave me alone!"

Seek Help

Reach out to nearby people for support. You can approach someone and say "Can you help me? This person is harassing me." If you're in immediate danger, call the police.

Document the Incident

If possible, make a note of the details of the harassment, including the time, place, and description of the harasser. This can be useful if you decide to report the incident.

Report the Incident

Report the harassment to local authorities or organizations that handle such complaints. Many cities have specific resources and hotlines for reporting street harassment.

Seek Support

Talk to friends, family, or a counselor about the experience. Emotional support is important to help you process and recover from the incident.

Resources

Contact Authorities

In cases of immediate danger, call emergency services (police 100).

For any emergency assistance please call women helpline number 112.

Report the abuse to local law enforcement to document the incidents and build a case if necessary.

If you or someone you know is experiencing sexual assault, reaching out for help and taking these steps can be life-changing and potentially life-saving.
