Teach children about stranger danger and safe adults

Children should understand the concept of "stranger danger" but also know that not all strangers are bad and that some may help in emergencies. Teach children to:

Recognize Unsafe Situations: Explain that they should never go anywhere with someone they donâ€™t know, even if the person says they know their parents or offers gifts or treats.

Identify Safe Adults: Help them identify safe adults they can approach if they are lost or in danger. These can include police officers, teachers, and store employees.

Have a Safety Code Word: Establish a family code word that someone must use if they are to pick up the child unexpectedly. This helps children identify when someone is truly sent by their parents.
