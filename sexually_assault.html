Sexual Assault Against Women

Sexual assault is any type of sexual activity or contact that occurs without the explicit consent of the recipient. It encompasses a range of actions, including but not limited to, rape, attempted rape, unwanted touching, and harassment.

What To Do If Someone Is Facing This Issue

Ensure Safety

Find a safe place: The immediate priority is to ensure safety. If in danger, move to a safe location as quickly as possible.

Seek Support

Contact trusted individuals: Reach out to friends, family, or someone trusted for emotional support and assistance.

Call a helpline: National and local sexual assault hotlines can provide confidential support and resources.

Medical Attention

Get medical care: Itâ€™s important to seek medical attention to address any injuries and for overall health. A healthcare professional can also conduct a forensic exam.

Reporting the Assault

Consider legal options: Reporting the assault to law enforcement is a personal decision. Legal assistance and advocacy services can help in making this decision and guide through the process.

Emotional and Psychological Support

Therapy and counseling: Professional counseling can provide a safe space to process emotions and begin the healing journey. Many organizations offer free or low-cost services for survivors.

Know Your Rights

Understand legal rights: Survivors have the right to receive medical care, to report the crime, and to receive support services. Familiarizing oneself with these rights can be empowering.

Resources

Contact Authorities

In cases of immediate danger, call emergency services (police 100).

For any emergency assistance please call women helpline number 112.

Report the abuse to local law enforcement to document the incidents and build a case if necessary.

If you or someone you know is experiencing sexual assault, reaching out for help and taking these steps can be life-changing and potentially life-saving.
